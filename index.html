About

Hi there!

My name is Aisling McCallion, I'm from Donegal in Ireland. I am currently living and studying in Limerick City. I am currently undertaking a master degree in Interaction & Experience Design at the University of Limerick.

I graduated from Fashion, Knitwear & Textile Design in 2017 from the Limerick School of Art & Design. From 2017 to 2019 I lived and worked as a Knitwear Designer & Shima Seiki Programmer on Inis Me√°in, the middle island of the Aran Islands. I developed a strong interest in heritage, fashion technology and nature in design while inhabiting the island. In 2019, I completed a Minor Award in Social Enterprise from the University of Limerick. During this year I was also a member of spacecraft Artist's Studios and became Co-director of the space in the beginning of 2020. At the studio, I developed a freelance knitwear consultant business while exploring potential new avenues for my journey with textiles. In August 2020, I moved to Kilcar in Co. Donegal. Here under the employment of Blarney Woollen Mills, I created a new brand for the factory in Kilcar, Baye. The project let me realise the accumulation of textile knowledge and skills I had gained throughout my education and experience.

My time working in industry helped me realise that my moral values of sustainability, circular design and participatory design were incompatible with a traditionalist fashion system. Eager to re-position myself, re-skill and further study design methodologies, I embarked on my masters journey.

I maintain a writing practice and engage with textiles through self-directed research and enjoyment! In my spare time, I love to be in nature or to be dancing at gigs.
